##Use Case: Return Radar in Action

## Meet Sarah - Product Manager at a Growing Startup

Sarah uses Pulse to manage three major initiatives:
- **Product Launch** (beta release in 2 weeks)
- **Q1 Planning** (strategic roadmap)
- **Mobile App Redesign** (ongoing UX improvements)

---

## The Scenario

**Monday, Nov 18**: Sarah is deep in Product Launch mode. She's:
- Reviewing landing page copy
- Coordinating with the design team
- Scheduling beta user interviews
- Last activity in Pulse: Adding notes from design review meeting

**Tuesday-Friday, Nov 19-22**: **Unexpected pivot** - a critical customer issue requires her full attention. She:
- Completely shifts focus to customer crisis management
- Doesn't open Pulse for 4 days
- Product Launch work goes dormant
- Meanwhile, her colleague Sarah Chen emails: "Need your input on the beta timeline"

**Monday, Nov 25**: Crisis resolved. Sarah opens Pulse.

---

## Without Return Radar

Sarah opens Pulse and sees:
- 47 total todos across all projects
- 12 calendar events this week
- 8 recent notes

**Her reaction**: *"Okay... what was I doing last week? What's urgent? Let me scroll through everything..."*

**Time spent**: 25 minutes re-orienting herself
- Checking each project manually
- Re-reading notes from last week
- Cross-referencing calendar with todos
- Realizing Sarah Chen's email about beta timeline

**Result**: Feels overwhelmed, misses that Product Launch has 2 overdue tasks

---

## The Key Difference

| Aspect | Without Return Radar | With Return Radar |
|--------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **Time to orient** | 25 minutes | 3 minutes |
| **Cognitive load** | High (must piece together context) | Low (context pre-assembled) |
| **Risk of missing urgent items** | High | Low |
| **Confidence in priorities** | Uncertain | Clear |
| **Feeling** | Overwhelmed | In control |

---

## What Sarah Notices

1. **It knew she was gone**: Didn't bother her with Return Radar on normal daily logins
2. **It prioritized correctly**: Product Launch at top (overdue + external mention)
3. **It surfaced the email mention**: She might have missed Sarah Chen's email
4. **It gave her a plan**: Clear action list, not just information
5. **It felt like a COO**: "Here's what needs your attention, here's why, here's what to do"

## Business Impact

For Sarah personally:
- Saves 20+ minutes per return from absence
- Fewer dropped balls on critical projects
- More confident decision-making
- Less stress about "what did I forget?"

For Pulse:
- Demonstrates proactive AI COO value
- Reduces churn (users feel supported, not overwhelmed)
- Increases engagement (users trust Pulse to track context)
- Clear differentiation from competitors
